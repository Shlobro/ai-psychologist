# Contradictions

## Purpose
Every person contains contradictions — ways in which they hold two opposing truths simultaneously, or ways in which their stated values and actual behavior point in different directions. Contradictions are not flaws to be resolved; they are the evidence of a complex person navigating a complex world. The most interesting and honest profiling lives precisely in these tensions rather than smoothing them over. This file documents the genuine contradictions that make this person irreducibly human.

## What to capture here
- Contradictions between stated values and observable behavior
- Ways in which they are simultaneously two opposite things (e.g., deeply generous and profoundly selfish)
- Contradictions between public presentation and private reality
- Things they believe intellectually that they do not feel emotionally, or vice versa
- Contradictions within their self-concept (things they claim to be that coexist with opposite qualities)
- Ways in which their desires and their choices consistently point in opposite directions
- Contradictions between their past self and current self that have not been integrated
- Places where they hold two incompatible beliefs simultaneously
- Areas where they apply different standards to themselves than to others
- Contradictions that are a source of pain or confusion for them
- Contradictions that they seem entirely unaware of
- How the contradictions fit together — the deeper coherence beneath the surface tension
