# Energy

## Purpose
What drains a person and what restores them is among the most practically predictive things you can know about them. Energy dynamics determine how they structure their time, what they can sustain, what they have learned to protect, what they have been taught to ignore, and what the cost of their current life is to their underlying system. Energy is not only about introversion and extroversion, though that is part of it. It is about which activities, relationships, environments, and inner states are genuinely generative — leaving them more than they came — and which extract more than they give.

## What to capture here
- The conditions under which they feel most alive, most energized, most like themselves
- The activities, interactions, or environments that reliably deplete them — even if they value them
- Their introversion/extroversion profile as it specifically relates to energy (not just personality preference)
- How long they can sustain different types of demands — social, cognitive, creative, physical — before needing recovery
- What their recovery looks like: what restores them, how long it takes, what disrupts it
- Whether they are chronically running an energy deficit and what sustains that pattern
- The relationship between their energy and their physical state: sleep, food, movement, illness
- The activities they engage in that they think should restore them but actually drain them — and vice versa
- Whether they have accurate self-awareness about their own energy needs or have spent years ignoring them
- The social or professional costs of managing their energy honestly versus the personal cost of not doing so
- How their energy patterns have changed across their life — what has become more or less draining over time

## Text-Only Profiling Notes
- Energy profile is directly askable and people usually have strong intuitions about it: "what leaves you feeling better than before, and what leaves you feeling worse?" is a simple and high-yield question
- Cross-reference with social/social_energy.md and preferences/rituals.md — energy shows up across multiple files and the consistent pattern across all of them is the signal
- Ask what they do when they have a completely free day and no obligations — the structure they choose (or the paralysis they experience) is an energy map
- Note what topics in conversation seem to energize them (longer, more vivid, more specific responses) versus what seems to flatten them (shorter, more perfunctory, more hedged responses)
- Ask about a period in their life when they felt most energized and what the conditions were — and separately, a period when they felt most depleted and what produced it
- Discrepancy between what they say energizes them and what they actually consistently make time for can reveal the gap between aspired-to and actual energy needs
