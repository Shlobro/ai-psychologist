# What They Hide

## Purpose
Every person maintains a gap between what they show the world and what they conceal. What someone hides — from others, and sometimes from themselves — is often the most psychologically significant material available. People hide what they are ashamed of, what they fear would make them unlovable, what conflicts with the image they need to maintain, and what they have not yet found language for. This file maps the terrain of concealment with care and without judgment, in the interest of understanding the whole person.

## What to capture here
- Things they hide from most people but reveal to a trusted few
- Things they appear to hide from everyone, including close relationships
- The aspects of themselves they are most ashamed of
- Behaviors or patterns they engage in privately that contradict their public persona
- Feelings they consistently suppress or deny having
- The truths about their life they find hardest to speak aloud
- Wounds or fears they have never disclosed to anyone
- Secrets they carry about their history
- Desires they consider too dangerous or shameful to express
- Ways they are dishonest or self-deceptive — and what that self-deception protects
- Things they hide from themselves through rationalization or avoidance
- What they believe would happen if others saw these hidden parts
- Whether the gap between public and private selves is shrinking or growing
