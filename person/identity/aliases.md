# Aliases and Alternate Names

## Purpose
The names a person goes by — and the contexts in which they use them — reveal layers of identity, history, and social navigation. A nickname from childhood carries different weight than a professional name or an online pseudonym. Aliases can reflect how someone compartmentalizes their life, how they have reinvented themselves, or what they want to hide. This file tracks the full range of names and handles associated with a person across different domains of their life.

## What to capture here
- All known nicknames and who uses them
- Childhood names or pet names used by family
- Professional name or pen name, if different from legal name
- Online usernames, handles, and screen names across platforms
- Names used in specific communities or subcultures
- Former names (e.g., before marriage, transition, or legal change)
- Names they have chosen for themselves versus names given by others
- Names they dislike or have asked people to stop using
- What each alias reveals about the context or relationship it belongs to
- Any aliases used for privacy, anonymity, or separation of identity
- Names that carry emotional significance — positive or negative
