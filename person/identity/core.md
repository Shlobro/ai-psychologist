# Core Identity

## Purpose
This file captures the fundamental facts and self-definitions that form the skeleton of who a person is. Core identity is the layer of self that remains relatively stable across contexts — the basic coordinates of a human life. Understanding these elements first provides an anchor for everything else in the profile: psychological patterns, relational dynamics, and behavioral tendencies only become legible once you know who you are actually looking at.

## What to capture here
- Full legal name and any preferred names or nicknames
- Date and place of birth
- Current age and life stage
- Gender identity and how they relate to it
- Sexual orientation
- Nationality, citizenship, and cultural background
- Ethnicity and how much it factors into their self-concept
- Primary language(s) and any secondary languages
- Current location and how long they have lived there
- Relationship status and living situation
- Occupation or primary role in the world
- How they introduce themselves to strangers — what they lead with
- The labels they voluntarily claim (e.g., "I'm a father first," "I'm an artist")
- Labels others apply to them that they resist or reject
- Any identity-related tensions or ambivalences (e.g., caught between two cultures)
