# Blind Spots

## Purpose
Blind spots are the places where a person's self-perception most diverges from reality — areas of weakness they cannot see, patterns they cannot recognize in themselves, or impacts they are unaware of having on others. Every person has them. Blind spots are often the mirror image of strengths: the very qualities that make someone effective also tend to produce characteristic failures they cannot see. Mapping blind spots is essential for a complete and honest profile, and it requires observing the gap between how someone presents and how they actually land.

## What to capture here
- Patterns of behavior the person does not recognize in themselves but are visible to others
- Recurring problems they attribute entirely to external causes
- The impact they have on others that they seem genuinely unaware of
- Skills or capacities they overestimate in themselves
- Skills or capacities they underestimate in themselves
- Areas of consistent failure they have not connected to their own contribution
- Cognitive biases they exhibit reliably without awareness
- The emotion they least recognize in themselves when it is present
- Social dynamics they misread with some regularity
- The quality in others that most irritates them — often a disowned aspect of themselves
- Areas where their intelligence does not compensate for emotional or perceptual gaps
- What people who care about them would most want them to see about themselves
