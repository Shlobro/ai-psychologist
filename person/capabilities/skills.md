# Skills

## Purpose
Skills are the concrete competencies a person has developed through deliberate effort, experience, or training. They represent where a person has chosen to invest their time and capacity, and they shape what is possible for them in the world. Skills also carry identity weight — people often build self-concept around what they can do. Documenting the full range of skills, from technical to interpersonal to physical, provides a clear picture of a person's practical competencies and their self-perception in relation to them.

## What to capture here
- Professional or vocational skills and their level of mastery
- Technical skills: languages, tools, software, instruments, crafts
- Interpersonal skills: listening, leadership, negotiation, teaching, care
- Physical skills: athletic, manual, embodied
- Creative skills: artistic, literary, musical, design-oriented
- Organizational and administrative skills
- Skills they are actively developing or trying to acquire
- Skills they learned in youth that remain even if rarely used
- Skills they are proud of versus skills they underrate
- Skills others recognize in them that they do not fully claim
- Skills that were acquired out of necessity versus passion
- The gap between their skill and their confidence in it
- Skills they wish they had developed and why they did not
