# Intelligence

## Purpose
Intelligence is multi-dimensional — it is not simply IQ or academic achievement, but the full range of cognitive and emotional capacities through which a person engages with the world. Howard Gardner's framework of multiple intelligences and Daniel Goleman's emotional intelligence are useful starting points, but the most useful approach is to observe how someone actually thinks and navigates complex situations. This file documents the texture of this person's intelligence across different domains.

## What to capture here
- Verbal and linguistic intelligence: how they use language, their vocabulary, their precision or expressiveness
- Logical-analytical intelligence: their capacity for systematic reasoning, abstraction, and problem-solving
- Emotional intelligence: self-awareness, empathy, emotional regulation, social navigation
- Social intelligence: reading rooms, understanding group dynamics, influencing others
- Spatial or visual intelligence: thinking in images, navigation, design
- Kinesthetic or bodily intelligence: movement, physical awareness, manual precision
- Musical or pattern-recognition intelligence
- Naturalistic intelligence: attunement to the natural world
- Intuitive intelligence: pattern recognition that operates faster than conscious reasoning
- Creative intelligence: the ability to generate novel connections and ideas
- The intelligence types they are most proud of
- The intelligence types they underestimate or overlook in themselves
- How they deploy intelligence differently across contexts
