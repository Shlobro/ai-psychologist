# Talents

## Purpose
Talents are distinct from skills: while skills are acquired through practice, talents are capacities that come with unusual ease — areas where a person seems naturally attuned, where effort produces outsized results, or where others recognize a gift even when the person themselves takes it for granted. Talents are often the result of both natural aptitude and deep early absorption. Identifying a person's genuine talents — especially those they may not fully recognize — provides insight into their potential and into what they might be being called toward.

## What to capture here
- Areas where they grasp things unusually quickly or deeply
- Capacities that feel effortless to them but difficult for others
- Things people consistently come to them for — what others recognize as their gift
- Natural aptitudes that were evident in childhood
- Talents that have been formally recognized (awards, professional success, praise)
- Talents they are aware of and cultivate
- Talents they possess but discount or are embarrassed by
- The difference between what they are talented at and what they are passionate about
- Latent or undeveloped talents that circumstances have not yet drawn out
- Whether their life choices have allowed their talents to flourish
- Talents that serve them in surprising contexts
- Talents they wish they had versus the ones they actually possess
