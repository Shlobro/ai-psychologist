# Humor

## Purpose
Humor is one of the most revealing dimensions of a person's character. What makes someone laugh, what kind of humor they deploy, and what they find offensive or unfunny all expose their worldview, their anxieties, their relationship to power, and their degree of psychological safety. Humor also functions as a social tool — a way of connecting, deflecting, dominating, or expressing what cannot otherwise be said. A person's sense of humor is one of the most intimate things you can know about them.

## What to capture here
- The style of humor they most often employ: irony, absurdism, wordplay, self-deprecation, observational, dark, physical
- What reliably makes them laugh — specific comedians, types of situations, kinds of jokes
- How their humor functions socially: bonding, diffusing tension, maintaining distance
- Whether they use humor as a defense mechanism
- The humor they find offensive or in poor taste and why
- Whether they are able to laugh at themselves
- Whether their humor punches up, sideways, or down
- How their humor changes in different social contexts
- Whether they are funnier in private than in public, or the reverse
- The role humor plays in their closest relationships
- Memories of moments when humor failed them or got them in trouble
- What their humor reveals about how they actually see the world
