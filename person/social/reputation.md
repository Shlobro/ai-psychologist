# Reputation

## Purpose
Reputation is the collective social perception of who a person is — the story others tell about them when they are not in the room. It is not the same as character: reputation is what has been communicated and received through behavior over time in specific social contexts. Understanding someone's reputation (across different communities and relationships) and the degree to which it matches who they actually are is a crucial part of understanding both their social reality and the gap between their inner and outer lives.

## What to capture here
- How they are generally known in their professional context
- How they are generally known in their personal and social life
- The specific traits that consistently come up when others describe them
- The reputation they are aware of and actively cultivate
- The reputation they have that they are unaware of or would be surprised by
- How their reputation varies across different communities and contexts
- The gap between their self-perception and how others actually see them
- What aspects of their reputation they care deeply about protecting
- Reputational damage that has occurred and how it happened
- How they respond to threats to their reputation
- The reputation they want to build or recover
- Whether their reputation is earned or exaggerated in either direction
- Stories or events that defined public perception of them
