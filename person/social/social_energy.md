# Social Energy

## Purpose
Social energy describes how a person relates to the demands of social interaction at a physiological and psychological level — not whether they like people, but whether people energize or drain them, and in what doses and configurations. It is more nuanced than the introvert/extrovert binary suggests. Understanding someone's social energy patterns is essential to understanding how they structure their lives, what they need to recover, and what they find overwhelming versus vitalizing.

## What to capture here
- Basic orientation: does social interaction tend to energize or deplete them
- The optimal number of people and type of interaction for them to feel good
- What kinds of social situations they find most vitalizing
- What kinds of social situations feel most exhausting or stressful
- How much solitude they need and how they use it
- How they behave at parties or large gatherings
- How they behave in intimate one-on-one conversations
- How their social energy changes with familiarity — easier with strangers or with known people
- How long they can sustain social engagement before needing to retreat
- How their social energy has shifted over time (more or less social with age)
- What they do to restore themselves after social exertion
- Whether they experience loneliness, and in what conditions
- How their social energy requirements affect their relationships and commitments
