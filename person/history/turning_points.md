# Turning Points

## Purpose
A life is not a smooth arc — it is punctuated by moments of rupture and redirection. Turning points are the events, decisions, or encounters that fundamentally altered the course of a person's life or the content of who they are. They may be dramatic (a death, a diagnosis, a sudden departure) or quiet (a book read at the right moment, a conversation that reframed everything). Identifying a person's turning points reveals what has the power to move them, and it shows the fault lines between the person they were and the person they became.

## What to capture here
- The events they themselves identify as life-changing
- Turning points they may not recognize as such but that clearly shifted their trajectory
- The before-and-after quality of each turning point — what changed specifically
- Decisions they made at crossroads and what they chose and what they gave up
- Encounters with people who permanently altered their direction
- Moments of loss, grief, or crisis that reconfigured their priorities
- Moments of unexpected opportunity that they seized or failed to seize
- Turning points that were chosen versus those that were imposed
- How they narrate these moments now — with gratitude, regret, pride, or confusion
- The turning points still in progress: current thresholds they are standing at
- What each turning point reveals about their deepest values and fears
