# Adolescence

## Purpose
Adolescence is when identity first becomes a conscious project. It is the crucible in which social belonging, sexuality, values, and self-concept are tested and formed. The experiences of this period — the social hierarchies navigated, the first loves and heartbreaks, the rebellion or conformity, the dawning awareness of one's place in the world — leave deep imprints. Many of the social anxieties, relational patterns, and identity struggles adults carry were first encoded during these years.

## What to capture here
- Social experience: where they fit in the peer hierarchy, their friend group, their social role
- Experience of belonging or exclusion: were they popular, marginal, bullied, invisible
- Academic experience and what it meant to them
- Early romantic and sexual experiences and how they were received
- Relationship to authority: rebellious, compliant, avoidant, strategic
- The emergence of personal values, political awareness, or worldview
- Physical development and how they experienced it
- Key influences during this period: people, books, music, movements
- Defining achievements or failures from this period
- How they were seen versus how they saw themselves
- The identities they tried on and the one that eventually stuck
- What they were like at 16 — how similar or different from today
- Unfinished business from adolescence that persists into adulthood
