# Adulthood

## Purpose
Adulthood is where the patterns established in earlier life are either reinforced or challenged by real-world consequences. The choices made in adulthood — careers, relationships, places to live, risks taken or avoided — are the most voluminous evidence available for understanding a person. Adulthood also brings new pressures: financial responsibility, mortality, the weight of sustained commitments, and the gradual confrontation with who one actually is versus who one hoped to be. This file tracks the broad arc and key chapters of adult life.

## What to capture here
- Major phases or chapters of adult life and what characterized each
- Key decisions made and the values or fears that drove them
- Career path and how it evolved — linear, chaotic, deliberate, accidental
- Major relationships and their timelines
- Where they have lived and why those choices were made
- How their sense of self has evolved through adulthood
- The gap between who they imagined they would be and who they became
- Major losses endured in adulthood and how they were processed
- Moments of significant personal growth or transformation
- The version of themselves they are living now and how they feel about it
- Ongoing chapters and unresolved threads
- How they relate to their younger self — with compassion, contempt, or nostalgia
