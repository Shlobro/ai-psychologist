# Traumas

## Purpose
Trauma is an experience that overwhelms a person's capacity to integrate what happened — leaving fragments of the event lodged in the nervous system, distorting perception and behavior in ways that can persist for decades. Trauma is not defined by the severity of an event in objective terms but by its impact on the individual. This file documents traumatic experiences and their ongoing effects with the understanding that trauma is not the whole story of a person — but it is often the hidden infrastructure underneath much of their behavior. This file should be approached with great care.

## What to capture here
- Single-incident traumas: accidents, assaults, sudden loss, acute medical events
- Complex or developmental traumas: prolonged exposure to harm, neglect, or instability
- Vicarious traumas: witnessing suffering of others or inheriting family trauma
- Collective traumas: historical, cultural, or generational wounds
- How each trauma presented: did it produce numbness, hypervigilance, dissociation, rage
- Symptoms or patterns that appear to be trauma responses
- Whether the trauma has been named, processed, or remains unacknowledged
- The degree to which trauma has been integrated versus still actively dysregulating
- Trauma triggers: situations, sensations, or interactions that activate old responses
- The coping strategies (healthy and unhealthy) that emerged from trauma
- How trauma has affected relationships, identity, and self-worth
- Evidence of post-traumatic growth alongside ongoing difficulty
