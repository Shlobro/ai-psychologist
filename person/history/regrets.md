# Regrets

## Purpose
Regrets are the photographic negatives of a person's values — they show, in silhouette, what mattered most by revealing what a person wishes they had done differently. They are also a window into the gap between who someone aspired to be and who they actually were in the moment. How a person carries their regrets — whether with self-compassion or self-punishment, whether they have learned from them or are paralyzed by them — reveals a great deal about emotional maturity and psychological health.

## What to capture here
- Things they regret doing: actions taken, words spoken, choices made
- Things they regret not doing: risks not taken, words left unsaid, paths not walked
- Regrets about how they treated specific people
- Professional or creative regrets: opportunities missed, work abandoned
- Regrets about who they were at a certain time in their life
- How much time and energy they spend in regret versus how lightly they hold it
- Regrets they have made peace with versus regrets that still sting acutely
- Whether regret motivates change or produces paralysis
- The regrets they would admit to versus the ones they rarely acknowledge
- What their pattern of regrets reveals about their deepest values
- How they talk about regret: with self-blame, with philosophical acceptance, or with deflection
- Whether they use regret constructively to inform current choices
