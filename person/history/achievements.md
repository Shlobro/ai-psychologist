# Achievements

## Purpose
Achievements reveal what a person is capable of when motivated, what they have chosen to invest in, and what kinds of success matter to them. But achievements are also psychological artifacts: they show what a person needed to prove, who they were proving it to, and how they felt once they succeeded. The gap between a person's achievements and their satisfaction with them is often more revealing than the achievements themselves. This file documents what this person has actually accomplished and what those accomplishments mean to them.

## What to capture here
- Major professional or academic accomplishments
- Creative works produced or completed
- Relationship and family milestones they are proud of
- Personal challenges overcome (health, addiction, fear, hardship)
- Skills mastered or expertise developed over time
- Recognition received: awards, praise, promotions, honors
- Achievements they are proud of but rarely mention
- Achievements they lead with in social contexts and what that signals
- The effort behind each achievement — what it cost them
- Whether they feel their achievements are truly their own or attribute them to luck or others
- How their achievements have shaped their self-concept
- Goals they worked toward that did not materialize and how they reconcile that
- What they still want to achieve and why
