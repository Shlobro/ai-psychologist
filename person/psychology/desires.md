# Desires

## Purpose
Desire is the engine of human motivation — it is what a person moves toward rather than away from. Understanding what someone truly wants, at every level from the mundane to the profound, illuminates the logic behind their choices and their disappointments. Surface desires (money, status, comfort) often conceal deeper ones (security, love, significance). This file documents the full landscape of what this person wants and hungers for, which is inseparable from understanding who they are.

## What to capture here
- Immediate, practical desires: what they want right now in their life
- Longer-term goals and ambitions
- Relational desires: the kind of connection, love, or belonging they seek
- Recognition desires: how they want to be seen, appreciated, or known
- Experiential desires: adventures, states of being, or feelings they chase
- Material desires and what those actually represent to them
- Creative or expressive desires: what they want to make or put into the world
- Desires they are ashamed of or would not admit publicly
- Desires they are not consciously aware of but that show in their behavior
- The gap between what they say they want and what they actually pursue
- What they would want if they gave themselves full permission
- The core desire that seems to underlie all the others
