# Self-Perception

## Purpose
Self-perception is the story a person tells about themselves — who they believe they are, where they rank, what they deserve, and how they fit into the world. It is distinct from their actual personality or abilities; it is the internal mirror they consult constantly. Self-perception shapes confidence, ambition, relationship choices, and emotional regulation. The gap between self-perception and reality — whether inflated, deflated, or simply distorted in particular directions — is often where the most interesting and consequential psychology lives.

## What to capture here
- Overall self-esteem: the baseline level of self-regard that persists across contexts
- Which domains they feel confident in versus deeply insecure about
- How they describe themselves when asked directly
- The hidden self-image that contradicts their public persona
- Whether their self-perception is relatively stable or highly contingent on external feedback
- How they handle praise: do they accept it, dismiss it, or become suspicious of it
- How they handle criticism: do they collapse, get defensive, or integrate it
- The self-narratives they return to repeatedly ("I'm someone who always...", "I never...")
- How their self-perception has shifted over time and what caused changes
- Areas of pronounced shame versus areas of pride
- Whether they are harder on themselves than on others, or the reverse
- How accurate their self-perception appears to be from the outside
