# Wounds

## Purpose
Psychological wounds are the places where life hurt this person in ways that left a lasting mark. They are not simply bad memories — they are experiences that reshaped how a person sees themselves and the world, often in ways that persist long after the original injury. Wounds are frequently the hidden root of defensive behaviors, relationship patterns, and self-sabotage. Mapping a person's wounds is not about blame or victimhood — it is about understanding why someone is the way they are and what they are still carrying. This file requires particular care and sensitivity.

## What to capture here
- Early attachment wounds: experiences of neglect, abandonment, or inconsistent care
- Wounds inflicted by specific people: parents, siblings, peers, partners
- Wounds inflicted by systems: institutions, communities, society
- Experiences of betrayal and how they affected the capacity to trust
- Experiences of humiliation and their effect on self-worth
- Losses that were never fully grieved
- Times when they were made to feel invisible, unwanted, or fundamentally wrong
- Wounds they are aware of and have language for
- Wounds that operate silently — visible in behavior but not named
- How the wounds have been adaptive (what they learned in order to survive)
- How the same wounds now create problems in adult life
- Whether these wounds have been addressed through therapy, reflection, or time
