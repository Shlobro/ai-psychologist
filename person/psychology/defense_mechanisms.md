# Defense Mechanisms

## Purpose
Defense mechanisms are the psychological strategies a person deploys — usually unconsciously — to protect themselves from anxiety, pain, or threats to their self-image. They are not weaknesses; they are learned adaptations, often originally brilliant ones. But they can become rigid, limiting, and a source of suffering when overused or applied indiscriminately. Understanding how someone defends themselves tells you exactly what they cannot yet tolerate, and it reveals the shape of their inner vulnerability. Defenses are best observed in moments of stress, conflict, or confrontation.

## What to capture here
- Primary defense mechanisms in use (e.g., repression, rationalization, projection, denial, intellectualization, dissociation, reaction formation, sublimation, humor)
- What specifically triggers the deployment of defenses
- How sophisticated or primitive their defenses tend to be
- Whether their defenses are ego-syntonic (they don't see them as problems) or ego-dystonic (they are aware and troubled)
- How defenses manifest in conversation: deflection, subject changes, sudden humor, shutdown
- Physical signs of defense activation: body language, tone shifts, going cold
- What they are defending against in each case
- How their defenses affect their relationships
- Situations or relationships where defenses come down
- Whether their defensive style has evolved over time
- The cost of their dominant defenses to themselves and others
