# Core Beliefs

## Purpose
Core beliefs are the deep, often unexamined convictions a person holds about themselves, other people, and the world. They are the operating system running beneath conscious thought — shaping what someone notices, how they interpret events, and what they expect. Many core beliefs are formed in childhood and never consciously revised. Identifying them is essential to understanding why a person repeatedly makes the same choices, attracts the same situations, or struggles in the same ways. These beliefs are rarely stated outright; they must often be inferred from patterns of behavior.

## What to capture here
- What they believe about their own worth and deservingness
- What they believe about other people — trustworthy or dangerous, generous or selfish
- What they believe about the world — fair or arbitrary, abundant or scarce
- Beliefs about love: whether it is unconditional, whether it must be earned, whether it lasts
- Beliefs about success: whether it is attainable for them, what it requires, whether they deserve it
- Beliefs about change: whether people can change, whether they themselves can change
- Beliefs about suffering: whether it is meaningful, avoidable, or deserved
- Inherited beliefs from family, culture, or religion that they have never examined
- Beliefs they consciously hold versus ones revealed by their behavior
- Contradictions between stated beliefs and lived beliefs
- The single most foundational belief that underlies much of their behavior
