# Fears

## Purpose
Fear is one of the most powerful organizers of human behavior. What a person fears tells you what they are protecting, what they have already lost, and what they believe they cannot survive. Fears operate at multiple levels: surface phobias, social fears, existential dread, and the deep terror of being truly known and rejected. Many of a person's most confusing or self-defeating behaviors become completely logical once you understand what they are trying to avoid. This file maps the full topology of what frightens this person.

## What to capture here
- Concrete, specific phobias (spiders, heights, flying, etc.)
- Social fears: rejection, humiliation, being ignored, being exposed
- Relational fears: abandonment, engulfment, betrayal, loneliness
- Identity fears: being ordinary, being a failure, losing themselves
- Existential fears: death, meaninglessness, loss of control, irrelevance
- Fear of specific emotions they cannot tolerate feeling
- What situations or types of people reliably trigger fear or anxiety
- How they typically respond to fear — fight, flight, freeze, or fawn
- Fears they are aware of versus fears that only show in their behavior
- Things they avoid that reveal hidden fears
- The fear beneath the fear — the deeper wound underneath surface anxieties
- How their fears have shaped their major life decisions
