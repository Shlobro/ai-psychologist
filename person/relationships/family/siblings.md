# Siblings

## Purpose
Siblings are the first peers — the relationships in which a person first learns to compete, cooperate, negotiate, and find their place in a social group. Birth order, perceived favoritism, shared and divergent experiences of the same home, and the particular chemistry between siblings all leave lasting marks. Sibling relationships can be sources of profound support or deep injury, and the roles assigned early in the family (the responsible one, the rebel, the golden child, the invisible one) often persist into adulthood in ways the person does not fully recognize.

## What to capture here
- Number of siblings, birth order, and age gaps
- The personality and life path of each sibling
- The quality of each sibling relationship across life stages
- What role this person played in the sibling system
- What role was assigned to each sibling in the family narrative
- Presence of rivalry, favoritism, or comparison by parents
- Shared family experiences and how each sibling processed them differently
- How the sibling relationships have evolved into adulthood
- Current closeness or distance from each sibling and why
- Ways in which sibling dynamics show up in other relationships
- Any sibling who was particularly formative — positively or negatively
- Unresolved conflicts or unspoken histories with siblings
- What they learned about themselves from their position in the sibling group
