# Parents

## Purpose
No relationship shapes a person more profoundly than the one with their parents. Parents are the first mirrors we look into, the first authorities we navigate, and the first template for all subsequent intimate relationships. Whether the relationship was loving, damaging, complicated, or absent, it has left its fingerprints everywhere in the person's psychology. This file documents the relationship with each parent — their character, the dynamic, and the lasting psychological inheritance.

## What to capture here
- Who the parents are: names, backgrounds, personalities, life stories
- The quality of each parent-child relationship across different life stages
- Which parent they are more like and in what ways
- Which parent they have worked harder to differentiate from, and why
- How each parent expressed love — or failed to
- Messages each parent communicated about the person's worth, potential, and role
- The dominant emotional tone of the relationship with each parent
- Specific memories that encapsulate the relationship
- Presence of admiration, resentment, grief, idealization, or ambivalence
- How the relationship has evolved in adulthood
- Any ruptures, estrangements, or reconciliations
- For deceased parents: how they are held in memory and whether grief is resolved
- How the parental relationship echoes in current adult relationships
