# Family Patterns

## Purpose
Families are systems, and systems repeat. The patterns that characterize a family — in communication, conflict, intimacy, power, roles, and coping — tend to replicate across generations and within individual members even when they are not aware of it. Identifying the family system's characteristic patterns is essential to understanding the water this person has always swum in: what they learned to call "normal," what invisible rules they follow, and which dynamics they unconsciously recreate in new relationships.

## What to capture here
- The dominant emotional climate of the family: enmeshed, avoidant, volatile, stoic
- Characteristic communication styles: direct or indirect, honest or evasive, warm or cold
- How the family handled conflict: openly, by suppression, through triangulation, with violence
- Patterns of loyalty and belonging: who is in and who is cast out and for what
- Roles that have appeared across generations (the caretaker, the scapegoat, the hero)
- Patterns around addiction, mental illness, or other recurring struggles
- The family's relationship to money, ambition, and class
- How love was and was not expressed across generations
- Intergenerational trauma that has not been fully metabolized
- The spoken and unspoken rules of the family system
- Which of these patterns this person has consciously worked to break
- Which patterns they have replicated without realizing it
- The family's mythology about itself — how it sees its own identity and history
