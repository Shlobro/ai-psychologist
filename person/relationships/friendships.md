# Friendships

## Purpose
Friendships are the relationships a person chooses without the biological imperatives of family or the romantic stakes of partnership. The quality, depth, and character of someone's friendships reveal their social values, their capacity for reciprocity, and the kind of connection they are able to sustain over time. Who a person counts as a true friend — and who counts them as one in return — is among the most honest indicators of their interpersonal character.

## What to capture here
- How many close friendships they have and how long they have lasted
- The character and qualities of their closest friends
- What they look for in a friend — what they are drawn to
- What kind of friend they are: reliable, fun, wise, present, inconsistent
- How they handle conflict within friendships
- The typical dynamics that develop in their close friendships
- Friends from different life stages and which have endured
- Whether their friend group is tight and stable or loose and rotating
- How they maintain friendships over time and distance
- Their capacity for depth and vulnerability in friendships
- Friendships that ended and why — by drift, by betrayal, by choice
- Whether they have more one close friend or a broader social web
- Gender dynamics in their friendships: who they are closest to and why
- How friendships satisfy (or fail to satisfy) needs not met in other relationships
