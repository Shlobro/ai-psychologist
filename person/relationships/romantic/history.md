# Romantic History

## Purpose
The arc of a person's romantic history is among the richest sources of data available for psychological profiling. Each relationship they have entered, sustained, and left (or been left by) reveals what they are drawn to, what they can give, what they cannot tolerate, and what they keep repeating. Patterns across relationships — in the types of partners chosen, the reasons things ended, the roles played — are often more diagnostic than any single relationship in isolation.

## What to capture here
- Significant romantic relationships in chronological order
- The character and key qualities of each partner
- What attracted them to each partner initially
- The dynamic that developed over time in each relationship
- How each relationship ended and who initiated the ending
- What they believe each relationship was ultimately about
- Patterns across partners: similar traits, similar problems, similar outcomes
- The longest relationship and what sustained it for that long
- The most significant relationship and what made it so
- The most damaging relationship and its lasting effects
- What they have learned (or failed to learn) from their romantic history
- Any relationships they idealize or mythologize and why
- How they talk about ex-partners — with bitterness, fondness, neutrality, or silence
- The role they typically occupy in relationships: pursuer or distancer, caretaker or receiver
