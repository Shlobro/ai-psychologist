# Current Romantic Relationship

## Purpose
The current romantic relationship (or the state of being single and what that means for this person) is the live expression of everything in their relational history and attachment system. It is where patterns play out in real time. Understanding the current relationship — its health, its dynamics, its satisfactions and tensions — provides immediate context for much of the person's present emotional life and psychological state.

## What to capture here
- Current relationship status: partnered, single, dating, separated, widowed
- If partnered: who the partner is, how they met, how long they have been together
- The quality of the current relationship: its strengths, its tensions, its unresolved issues
- How this person behaves as a partner: their strengths and their struggles
- The power dynamic in the relationship
- How needs are communicated and whether they are met
- How conflict is handled between them
- The level of emotional and physical intimacy and whether it is satisfying
- Whether the relationship is growing or stagnating
- What the person says they want from the relationship versus what they actually pursue
- If single: how they feel about it — free, lonely, at peace, avoidant
- If single: what is keeping them from partnership, if they want it
- Future intentions or uncertainty about the relationship
- How the current situation compares to what they imagined their love life would be
