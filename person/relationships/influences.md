# Influences

## Purpose
A person is not only the product of their experiences but of the minds and works they have allowed to enter and reshape them. Influences — the people, books, ideas, teachers, artists, movements, and thinkers that have left a genuine mark — reveal what a person aspires to, what speaks to them at the level of identity, and what lenses they use to interpret the world. Tracking influences helps explain where specific beliefs, aesthetic sensibilities, and ways of being came from.

## What to capture here
- People who have profoundly influenced them: mentors, teachers, friends, public figures
- Books, films, albums, or artworks that changed how they see the world
- Intellectual or philosophical traditions they have been shaped by
- Religious or spiritual teachers and traditions
- Cultural movements or historical moments that formed them
- Online communities or creators who have shaped their thinking
- Influences they would proudly claim versus those they are ambivalent about
- Influences from early life that they have since outgrown or revised
- Current influences: what they are reading, watching, or absorbing now
- The through-line across their influences — what do their chosen influences have in common
- Influences that have conflicted with one another and produced internal tension
- Who or what they wish had influenced them more
- The influence they would most resist acknowledging
