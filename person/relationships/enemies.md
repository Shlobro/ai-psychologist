# Enemies and Antagonists

## Purpose
Enemies — people a person is in genuine conflict with, deeply distrusts, or has been hurt by — are psychologically revealing in ways that admirers often are not. Who someone opposes, why they oppose them, and how they conduct that opposition tells you about their values, their wounds, and their capacity for fairness when they feel wronged. The enemies a person makes and keeps also reveal something about who they are: what they are unwilling to compromise on, and what lines they draw.

## What to capture here
- Known individuals they are in active conflict with
- People they deeply distrust or regard as adversaries
- Former friends or allies turned antagonists, and the story of the rupture
- People they are consciously or unconsciously in competition with
- Whether enmity tends to be mutual or largely one-directional
- How they speak about people they dislike: with contempt, hurt, or cold precision
- Whether they hold grudges or tend to move on
- Patterns in the type of person who becomes their enemy
- How much of their mental and emotional energy goes toward opposition
- Whether their characterization of enemies seems fair, exaggerated, or paranoid
- What virtues or wounds are being defended in each enmity
- Whether any enmities have been resolved and how
