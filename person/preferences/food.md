# Food

## Purpose
Food is never purely nutritional — it is cultural, emotional, social, and deeply personal. How a person eats, what they eat, and their relationship to eating reveals their relationship to pleasure, control, nourishment, identity, and embodiment. Food preferences carry the flavors of childhood, cultural heritage, and emotional memory. Eating habits can also reflect anxiety, comfort-seeking, discipline, indulgence, or anything in between. This file documents the full texture of a person's relationship to food.

## What to capture here
- Favorite foods and cuisines — the things they return to reliably
- Foods they absolutely will not eat and the reason (if known)
- Dietary choices and restrictions: vegetarian, vegan, allergies, religious rules, preferences
- Their relationship to cooking: do they enjoy it, avoid it, find it meditative
- Whether food is primarily functional or a significant source of pleasure
- The emotional role of food: comfort eating, restriction, social bonding
- Foods tied to memory, culture, or family identity
- Their relationship to eating in social contexts versus alone
- Any history of difficult or disordered relationships with food
- Their openness to trying new cuisines or dishes
- Relationship to alcohol, coffee, or other food-adjacent substances
- What a typical day of eating looks like
- What their food choices reveal about identity, class, culture, or values
