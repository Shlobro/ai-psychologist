# Rituals and Routines

## Purpose
Rituals and routines are the recurring practices through which a person structures time, manages anxiety, accesses pleasure, and maintains a sense of self. They are distinct from mere habits in that they often carry emotional or symbolic significance — they are not just efficient but meaningful. The rituals someone maintains (and the ones they have abandoned) reveal what they need to function, what gives them comfort, and how they relate to regularity and change.

## What to capture here
- Morning routine and how important it is to them
- Evening or wind-down rituals
- Weekly rhythms and recurring practices
- Personal rituals around significant transitions: seasons, birthdays, new years, grief
- Creative or productive rituals: what conditions are necessary for them to do their best work
- Social rituals: recurring gatherings, traditions with friends or family
- Rituals around food, exercise, or physical maintenance
- Private rituals that others may not know about
- Rituals that are self-soothing in nature — what they do when stressed or overwhelmed
- Rituals tied to identity or culture
- How they respond when their routines are disrupted — rigidly or flexibly
- Rituals they have recently adopted or let go
- What their rituals reveal about their deeper needs and psychological organization
