# Media

## Purpose
The media a person consumes — films, books, music, podcasts, television, games, social media — is a direct record of what captures their attention and imagination. Media choices reflect curiosity, aesthetic taste, emotional needs, intellectual interests, and the desire for escape or stimulation. What someone chooses to spend hours with is a highly reliable indicator of who they are, what they are working through, and what they love. The media that has moved or changed them is especially significant.

## What to capture here
- Favorite books and literary genres — what they reach for and what they avoid
- Favorite films and directors — the storytelling sensibilities they respond to
- Music they love: artists, genres, specific albums or songs with significance
- Television and streaming content they follow with investment
- Podcasts, video content, or audio they consume regularly
- Games or interactive media that occupy significant time or attention
- Their relationship to social media: which platforms, how much, how it feels
- News and information consumption: where they get it, how much, how it affects them
- The piece of media they would choose if stranded on a desert island
- Media that has changed their thinking or their life
- Media they consume when they need comfort
- Media they consume when they want to be challenged
- What their media diet reveals about their intellectual and emotional life
