# Dislikes

## Purpose
Dislikes are the photographic negative of a person's preferences — they reveal the flip side of what they value by showing what they find intolerable, irritating, or deeply unpleasant. Strong dislikes often carry psychological significance beyond mere taste: what annoys or repels a person frequently reflects their sensitivities, their wounds, or the things they most need to feel safe and in control. Paying attention to what someone cannot stand is as illuminating as paying attention to what they love.

## What to capture here
- Activities or situations they avoid or endure with difficulty
- Social dynamics that produce discomfort, irritation, or distress
- Environments that feel hostile, overstimulating, or draining
- Types of people who reliably irritate or repel them
- Intellectual or cultural content they find worthless or offensive
- Sensory aversions: sounds, textures, smells, or environments they cannot tolerate
- Behaviors in others that provoke a strong negative reaction
- Things that feel morally objectionable to them
- Pet peeves — the small things that disproportionately bother them
- Dislikes that seem connected to deeper wounds or fears
- Dislikes that have evolved — things they once loved and now cannot stand
- Dislikes they are embarrassed to admit
- What their dislikes reveal about their temperament and values
