# Aesthetics

## Purpose
Aesthetic sensibility — the quality and style of beauty a person is drawn to — is a deeply personal and often underexamined dimension of character. It reflects a person's relationship to form, feeling, and meaning. Aesthetic preferences manifest across every domain of life: the spaces people inhabit, the clothes they wear, the art they collect, the words they love. A person's aesthetic is a kind of philosophy expressed through sensation and form rather than argument. Understanding it provides insight into who they are that cannot be accessed through direct questioning.

## What to capture here
- Visual aesthetics: the styles, color palettes, and forms they find beautiful
- Their relationship to minimalism versus maximalism, order versus chaos, clean versus rough
- Architectural and interior design preferences
- Fashion and clothing aesthetics — the look they gravitate toward
- The artists, designers, photographers, or filmmakers whose visual world resonates with them
- Musical aesthetics: not just genre but the quality and texture of sound they love
- Literary aesthetics: prose style, narrative form, the quality of language they are drawn to
- How their personal aesthetic extends (or doesn't) into how they present themselves
- Whether their aesthetic is consistent across domains or shifts depending on context
- Aesthetic experiences that have moved them — beauty that felt almost unbearable
- The aesthetic they aspire to versus the one they actually inhabit
- What their aesthetic sensibility reveals about their inner life
