# Health

## Purpose
Physical health is the substrate on which everything else runs. Chronic illness, pain, fatigue, disability, or excellent physical vitality all profoundly shape how a person navigates the world — what is possible for them, what demands their attention, what they fear. Health history also often contains psychological significance: the body keeps the score of stress, grief, and trauma. Understanding a person's physical health in full is necessary for a complete picture.

## What to capture here
- Current state of physical health: generally healthy, managing conditions, struggling
- Diagnosed chronic conditions and their daily impact
- Significant past medical events: surgeries, serious illnesses, hospitalizations
- Mental health: diagnosed conditions, treatment history, current management
- Medication and its role in daily functioning
- Sleep: quantity, quality, patterns, and the effect of disruption
- Energy levels across the day and week
- How they relate to their own health: proactive, avoidant, anxious, fatalistic
- Their relationship to doctors and the medical system
- Any health concerns that occupy significant mental real estate
- The effect of health on their life choices, relationships, or identity
- Family health history and how aware or concerned they are about it
- Habits that support or undermine their health
