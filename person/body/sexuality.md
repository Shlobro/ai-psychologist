# Sexuality

## Purpose
Sexuality is a fundamental dimension of human experience that encompasses desire, intimacy, identity, and embodiment. It is shaped by biology, psychology, culture, and history, and it intersects with almost every other dimension of a person's life. Understanding someone's sexuality — not simply their orientation, but the full texture of their erotic and intimate life — is important for a complete profile. This file should be approached with sensitivity, discretion, and respect for complexity.

## What to capture here
- Sexual orientation and how they identify and experience it
- How central sexuality is to their sense of self and identity
- Their sexual history in broad strokes: formative experiences, significant relationships
- How they experience desire: frequency, intensity, what it is directed toward
- Their relationship to physical intimacy: comfortable, anxious, avoidant, enthusiastic
- Whether their sexual self is integrated or compartmentalized from the rest of their life
- How they communicate about sex and desire with partners
- Cultural, religious, or familial influences on their relationship to sexuality
- Any experiences that have complicated or shaped their sexual life
- The relationship between emotional intimacy and sexual desire for them
- How their sexuality has evolved over time
- Any gap between their private desires and what they allow themselves to express
- How they feel about their own sexuality: comfortable, conflicted, curious, settled
