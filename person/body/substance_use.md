# Substance Use

## Purpose
Substance use — including alcohol, caffeine, cannabis, prescription medications, and other drugs — is a significant part of many people's lives and carries important implications for health, psychology, relationships, and daily functioning. The substances someone uses (or avoids), the role those substances play, and the relationship between use and emotional states can reveal a great deal about how a person manages stress, seeks pleasure, or numbs difficulty. This file documents substance use without judgment, in the interest of a complete and honest profile.

## What to capture here
- Relationship to alcohol: frequency, quantity, contexts of use, emotional role
- Relationship to cannabis and other recreational substances
- Use of stimulants: caffeine in particular, and any others
- Any history of problematic or addictive use
- Current relationship to substances: controlled, habitual, abstinent, in recovery
- Whether substance use has caused or contributed to significant problems
- Substances they use to cope with stress, anxiety, or emotional pain
- Substances they use for pleasure, social bonding, or creative enhancement
- The identity weight of substance use or abstinence (e.g., sobriety as identity)
- Family history of addiction or problematic use and how aware they are of it
- Any shifts in substance use across different life periods
- What their substance use patterns reveal about their emotional regulation
- How honestly they represent their substance use to themselves and others
