# Person Profile — Index

## Profiling Constraint: Text-Only Medium

**All profiling in this system is conducted through text interaction only.** The profiler has no direct contact with the subject. They cannot see them, hear them, or observe them in person. Every piece of information in every file derives exclusively from text — from questions asked and answers received in written form.

This constraint changes how the entire system should be used:

- **Observable dimensions become inferential.** Appearance, body language, vocal tone, and physical presence cannot be directly observed. They must be elicited through self-report or inferred from the texture of self-description.
- **Text behavior is primary data.** What the subject volunteers, what they avoid, the words they choose, how long they write, how they open and close thoughts, what topics produce brief versus elaborate responses — all of this is first-order evidence, not secondary inference.
- **Self-report has a specific bias.** Everything known about this person is filtered through their own self-perception and self-presentation. This is not a limitation to be lamented but a feature to be used: how someone chooses to describe themselves is a psychological document in its own right.
- **Patterns across exchanges matter more than single answers.** A single response can be performance; consistent behavior across many questions, topics, and moods is character.
- **Silences and omissions are data.** Topics that never arise, questions deflected, answers that orbit the actual question — these negative spaces are as informative as what is directly said.
- **Every file's "Text-Only Profiling Notes" section explains specifically how to extract that dimension through text.** Read those sections before approaching any profiling conversation.

## Purpose

This system is a structured framework for building a deep, honest, and multi-dimensional profile of a real human being. It is designed on the premise that understanding a person requires more than a collection of facts — it requires mapping the full territory of their inner and outer life: who they are at their core, what shaped them, what drives and frightens them, how they relate to others, and where their life is heading.

The files in this system are organized into thematic domains, each addressing a different dimension of personhood. No single file tells the whole story. The richest understanding comes from reading across files — noticing how patterns in psychology show up in relationships, how history echoes in present behavior, how stated values diverge from enacted ones. The goal is not a summary but a living, accumulating map of a person as they actually are.

**How to use this system:**
- Fill in what you know; leave blank what you do not
- Revisit and revise files as your understanding deepens
- Pay particular attention to contradictions — they are not mistakes, they are data
- Treat the `meta/` folder as the synthesis layer: it is where the whole becomes more than the sum of its parts
- Approach all files, especially sensitive ones, with care, honesty, and respect for complexity

## Folders

| Folder | Description |
|---|---|
| `identity/` | The basic coordinates of who this person is: name, appearance, and the names they go by |
| `psychology/` | The interior architecture: personality, beliefs, fears, desires, wounds, defenses, shame, and motivation |
| `history/` | The chronological record: childhood through adulthood, turning points, traumas, regrets, and cultural context |
| `relationships/` | The relational map: family, romance, friendship, enemies, formative influences, and the relationship with self |
| `inner_world/` | The ideological and spiritual interior: values, morality, politics, mythology, and obsessions |
| `preferences/` | The texture of taste and daily pleasure: likes, dislikes, aesthetics, food, media, and rituals |
| `capabilities/` | What this person can do: skills, intelligence, talents, and blind spots |
| `social/` | How they move through the world with others: communication, humor, energy, power, and conflict |
| `body/` | The physical self: health, embodiment, sexuality, substance use, and sleep |
| `external/` | The outer life: career, finances, environment, daily existence, and technology |
| `neurology/` | The neurological architecture: ADHD/autism spectrum traits, sensory profile, and memory |
| `language/` | The linguistic dimension: vocabulary, communication patterns, what they avoid saying, and code switching |
| `creativity/` | The creative life: relationship to creativity, creative blocks, and artistic influences |
| `time/` | Temporal psychology: time orientation, relationship to change, and urgency |
| `money_psychology/` | The psychology of money: emotional relationship to money and spending as behavior |
| `meta/` | The synthesis layer: contradictions, hidden dimensions, masks, energy, developmental arc, and open questions |
