# Politics

## Purpose
Political views are a compressed expression of how a person sees human nature, social organization, justice, and power. They are rarely purely intellectual — they are typically rooted in identity, experience, emotion, and in-group membership. Understanding a person's political worldview (including its origins and its emotional charge) reveals what they fear about society, what they believe people are owed, who they consider "us" versus "them," and how they think change happens. Politics is also a domain where social performance and genuine belief often diverge.

## What to capture here
- Their general political orientation and how they describe it
- Specific issues they care most about and why
- The emotional intensity of their political engagement
- Where their political views came from: family, experience, community, reading
- How much their politics are performative versus deeply held and thought-through
- Whether their politics are consistent across domains or selective
- How they engage with people who disagree with them politically
- Their relationship to political institutions: trust, skepticism, cynicism, activism
- Any significant shifts in their political views and what drove them
- The political views they hold privately that they moderate in public
- Whether they translate political beliefs into action
- How political identity intersects with other aspects of their identity
- The political figure or movement they admire most and what that reveals
