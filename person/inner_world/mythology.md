# Personal Mythology

## Purpose
Every person operates within a personal mythology — a set of recurring stories, symbols, metaphors, and narrative arcs through which they interpret their own life. These myths give shape and meaning to experience: they cast the person as a particular kind of protagonist, assign roles to the people around them, and suggest what the story is ultimately about. Personal mythology is often unconscious, expressed through the stories someone returns to repeatedly, the heroes they identify with, and the recurring themes in how they describe their life.

## What to capture here
- The narrative arc they place themselves in: hero's journey, tragedy, redemption story, survivor tale
- The recurring stories they tell about themselves and what each reveals
- Myths, archetypes, fictional characters, or historical figures they deeply identify with
- The metaphors they use most frequently to describe their life or their struggles
- The story they tell about why they are the way they are
- The antagonist in their life story — who or what plays the villain
- Whether they see themselves as the cause of their story or as acted upon by forces
- Themes that appear again and again across the chapters of their life
- The story they believe they are currently in the middle of
- The ending they are working toward — consciously or not
- Myths or stories they cling to that are no longer serving them
- Moments when reality has forced them to revise their mythology
