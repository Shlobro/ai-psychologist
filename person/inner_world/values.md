# Values

## Purpose
Values are the principles a person uses to decide what is worth doing, what is worth protecting, and what is worth sacrificing other things for. They are the backbone of character. But values are best understood through behavior, not declaration — what a person says they value and what they actually prioritize when forced to choose are often different things. This file aims to document both the espoused values and the enacted values, and to understand the relationship between them.

## What to capture here
- The values they name when asked: freedom, loyalty, honesty, family, achievement, etc.
- The values demonstrated by how they actually spend time, money, and energy
- The hierarchy of values — which takes precedence when two values conflict
- Values that are deeply personal versus values absorbed from culture or family
- Values that have shifted over time and what caused those shifts
- Values they hold privately that they do not broadcast publicly
- Values they broadcast publicly that are not fully reflected in behavior
- Moral or ethical lines they claim they would never cross — and whether evidence supports that
- Values they admire in others but do not yet embody in themselves
- The value that feels most central to their sense of identity
- How their values create conflict with the people around them
- What it costs them to live according to their values
